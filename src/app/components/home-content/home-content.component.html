About Naruto

Naruto is a Shonen (teen male oriented) animated series and manga (Japanese comic) created by Masashi Kishimoto. Both the anime and the manga are ongoing in Japan at the moment. The series has been licensed abroad in various countries.

You can find Naruto manga on American shelves and the animated series airing in the Philippines. The show takes place in a world populated by ninja villages. The ninja in the series serve as the armies for the countries that inhabit the world. Most of the countries have their own Hidden Village, which serves to train and manage the ninja of the country.

Also the ninja in the series are able to utilize jutsu techniques, which are the secrecy, body and illusion arts of the ninja. These allow the ninja to perform many amazing skills such as the manipulation of the surrounding elements. The main character of the series is Uzumaki Naruto, a young boy who dreams of becoming the leader of his Hidden Village.

It will be difficult though for Naruto, because when he was a baby an evil demon was placed inside him to stop its rampage. As Naruto grew the townspeople saw Naruto himself as the demon, even though he was merely its container. When the series begins the reader and viewer will begin their journey with Naruto on his path to become respected by the village.

The Naruto manga is drawn by Masashi Kishimoto. Kishimoto was born in the Okayama Prefecture, Japan in 1974. He gained early acclaim with his work Karakuri. Later in 1997 he would develop an early incarnation of Naruto for Akamaru Jump. The manga would formally begin in 1999 in Weekly Shonen Jump, gaining a large fan following. Kishimoto is not alone in drawing the series, he has assistants who share various drawing duties.

The Story

Naruto follows the story of a young, energetic, and mischievous ninja named Naruto Uzumaki, who is determined to earn the respect of his village, Konohagakure, and become its leader, the Hokage. Orphaned at birth, Naruto carries the burden of being the host of the Nine-Tails Fox, a powerful demon that attacked the village years ago. This causes villagers to fear and shun him. Throughout his journey, Naruto forms bonds with his friends, including Sasuke Uchiha and Sakura Haruno, while facing various challenges and enemies, including rogue ninjas and the mysterious criminal organization, Akatsuki. Along the way, Naruto learns the value of hard work, friendship, and never giving up on his dreams.

The story picks up two and a half years after the original Naruto series, with Naruto returning to the village after intensive training. Now more mature and skilled, he continues his quest to bring Sasuke back, who has left the village seeking power from the rogue ninja, Orochimaru. The saga escalates as the Akatsuki organization intensifies its efforts to capture the Tailed Beasts, including the Nine-Tails inside Naruto. Naruto faces intense battles and confronts the darkness in his own heart while continuing to struggle with the emotional scars left by his past. In this arc, themes of loss, revenge, and the burden of power dominate, leading to powerful battles between Naruto and various enemies, including his once-close friend Sasuke.
In the final stages of Naruto Shippuden, the truth behind the world's conflicts is revealed, with Madara Uchiha and the mysterious figure, Tobi, manipulating events to start the Fourth Great Ninja War. Naruto, now a formidable warrior, joins forces with his allies to stop the war and face the powerful foes who seek to control the world. Meanwhile, his rivalry and friendship with Sasuke come to a head, as the two ultimately face each other in a climactic battle. Naruto’s unwavering belief in the possibility of peace and redemption leads him to defeat the antagonists, and Sasuke, after realizing the destructive path he’s on, ultimately finds his own redemption. The series concludes with Naruto achieving his dream of becoming the Seventh Hokage, symbolizing the culmination of his growth, and the beginning of a new era of peace for the ninja world.
